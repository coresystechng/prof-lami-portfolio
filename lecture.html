Prof. Lami A. Nnamonu’s Inaugural Lecture at Joseph Sarwuan Tarka University, Makurdi

Pioneering sub-Saharan West Africa's Professor of Agrochemistry, from the Department of Environmental Sustainability, College of Physical Sciences, Joseph Sarwuan Tarka University Makurdi, (JOSTUM), Professor Lami Angela Nnamonu (PMP) has emphasized the need to uphold standard best practices in the application of agrochemicals to ensure environmental sustainability.

Professor Lami Nnamonu who is the Center Leader and Director, Institute of Procurement, Environmental and Social Standards (IPESS), JOSTUM made the call on Wednesday September 24, 2025, while delivering the 81st edition of JOSTUM's Inaugural Lecture series at Aper Aku Auditorium, North Core of the University.

Delivering on the topic, "From Molecules to Mindsets: Trailblazing Agrochemistry and Leading Change," the Agrochemist stated that, Agrochemistry plays a critical role in modern agriculture by providing solutions for improved nutrition and pest resistance, leading to increased food production. However, the introduction of Genetically Modified Organisms (GMOs) has sparked a significant debate regarding the safety and environmental impact, reflecting broader societal concerns about the consequences of agricultural practices. According to the Center Leader and Director, “As the global demand for food increases, agricultural chemistry continues to evolve, striving for environmentally friendly practices while addressing the pressing need for sustainable food production. The balance between innovation and ecological responsibility remains a key challenge for professionals in this field.”

Professor Nnamonu asserted that agrochemicals have adverse short- and long-term effects on human health but conventional pesticides can be made more environmentally friendly while still maintaining their effectiveness in protecting crops. She maintained that this can be achieved by, formulation improvements, application techniques, and chemical design and selection. Other viable ways are integration with biological systems, environmental safeguards and policy and market innovations.

Dwelling on leading change, the University Don stated that change is one of the constants in life, but leading change requires something more than just experiencing it- it demands vision, courage and commitment. She added that leading change for her has never been optional but a calling.

The Trailblazing Professor of Agrochemistry in sub-Saharan Africa highlighted her enormous milestones and support to teaching, Girls in Science, Technology, engineering and Mathematics (STEM), Enactus JOSTUM, the American Chemical Society as well as her leadership role under the Sustainable Procurement, Environmental and Social Standards Enhancement (SPESSE) project known at JOSTUM as Institute of Procurement, Environmental and Social Standards (IPESS). The Center Leader Submitted that under her stewardship, the University’s SPESSE Center of Excellence has not only reversed the trajectory but now stands as the best performing Center in the country; redefining what it means to execute donor-funded projects with precision, integrity and impact.

Professor Nnamonu further called for the formation of the not-for-profit Environmental Working Group (EWG) in Nigeria to conduct research and advocacy for public health and environmental protection with a focus on areas like chemical safety, clean energy, health food, and personal care products which will provide information to consumers, and push for industries and policy reforms. On exemplary leadership, the IPESS Director prayed for leaders of positive change, saying, the future does not belong to those who wait for change but those who lead it.

Earlier, the Vice Chancellor of JOSTUM, Engr Professor Isaac Nathaniel Itodo, thanked Professor Lami A. Nnamonu on her devotion, effort, and achievements to society and academia. The Vice Chancellor expressed confidence that the presentation will inspire and motivate attendees.
