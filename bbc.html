Prof. Lami Nnamonu Featured on BBC Gist Nigeria: A Journey of Passion, Science, and Societal Impact

Prof. Lami Nnamonu, a renowned Professor of Agrochemistry, STEM advocate, and Project Management Professional, was recently featured on the BBC program Gist Nigeria. This special feature offered a powerful glimpse into her remarkable journey—from her childhood experiences and early aspirations as a young girl in science, to her impactful career in teaching, research, mentoring, and project leadership.

In the interview, Prof. Nnamonu shared key insights on agricultural innovation, sustainable agrochemical practices, and the crucial role of STEM in advancing socio-economic development across Africa. Her story reflects a life driven by purpose, curiosity, and a deep commitment to using science to improve lives and safeguard the environment.

She continues to lead with passion and purpose, fostering meaningful research, championing evidence-based solutions, and mentoring the next generation of scientists and innovators—especially young women aspiring to build careers in science.

“This platform was more than a feature; it was an opportunity to lend my voice to issues that truly matter,” she reflected. “Science must go beyond the laboratory—it must be practical, inclusive, and transformative.”

Her story serves as a source of inspiration for emerging scientists and change-makers across the continent. We celebrate her achievements and applaud her continued efforts to push boundaries, inspire excellence, and drive meaningful change through science.
